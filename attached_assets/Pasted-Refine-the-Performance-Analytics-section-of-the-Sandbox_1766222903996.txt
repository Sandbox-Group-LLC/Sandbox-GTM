Refine the Performance → Analytics section of the Sandbox app to eliminate perceived redundancy across the four analytics pages while preserving all existing pages, routes, and data sources. This update is a clarity and intent refinement, not a feature removal or rebuild.

The goal is to ensure each analytics page answers one distinct GTM decision question, with no overlap in responsibility or metrics.

Guardrails

Do NOT remove any existing pages.

Do NOT rename routes or break existing logic.

Changes should be limited to metric placement, page intent, framing copy, and visual hierarchy.

Each page should feel like a different altitude, not a duplicate dashboard.

Page-by-Page Refinement Rules
1. GTM Overview (Executive Summary Only)

Purpose:
Answer “Is this program working at a glance?”

Changes:

Treat this page as a pure executive summary.

Show only rolled-up, directional indicators:

Acquisition (high-level)

Engagement (high-level)

Revenue (high-level)

Use scorecards, status indicators, or trend arrows only.

Do NOT show:

Funnels

Detailed attribution

Channel breakdowns

Lists (hot leads, attendees, etc.)

This page should summarize outcomes, not explain them.

2. Acquisition Health (Top-of-Funnel Diagnostics)

Purpose:
Answer “Are we attracting the right audience from the right channels?”

Changes:

This page exclusively owns:

Conversion rate

Audience quality / ICP match

Acquisition funnel

Channel performance

Source attribution

Emphasize audience quality and efficiency, not volume.

Do NOT show:

Revenue numbers

Pipeline metrics

Engagement or intent scoring

This page should feel like a demand-gen optimization workspace.

3. Engagement Signals (Intent & Sales Readiness)

Purpose:
Answer “Who is showing buying intent, and how?”

Changes:

This page exclusively owns:

Engagement rate

Intent score

Real-time activity

High-intent audience lists (“Hot Leads”)

Frame everything around behavioral signals and readiness.

Do NOT show:

ROI

Cost metrics

Channel acquisition data

This page should feel sales-adjacent and people-centric, not aggregate.

4. Revenue Snapshot (Outcome & Accountability Layer)

Purpose:
Answer “Did this program move pipeline or revenue?”

Changes:

Treat this as the verdict page.

This page exclusively owns:

Pipeline generated / influenced

Revenue influenced

Cost per lead / cost efficiency

Velocity or time-to-close impact

Do NOT show:

Raw engagement metrics

Attendance counts

Session duration

Channel acquisition details

Optional refinement:

Consider renaming this page to Revenue Impact to better reflect accountability and final outcomes.

Global UX Refinements

Add a one-line framing subtitle at the top of each page that states the decision it supports (e.g., “Are we attracting the right audience?”).

Ensure no individual metric appears on more than one analytics page, except in summarized form on GTM Overview.

Visually differentiate:

GTM Overview = summary

Acquisition Health = inputs

Engagement Signals = behavior

Revenue Snapshot = outcomes
